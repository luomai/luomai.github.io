I am an Associate Professor, equivalent to Reader in the UK system, in the [School of Informatics](https://www.ed.ac.uk/informatics) at the [University of Edinburgh](https://www.ed.ac.uk), where I lead the [Large-Scale Machine Learning Systems Group](/#group). I also co-lead the [UK EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training in Machine Learning Systems](https://mlsyscdt.github.io/) and a [UK ARIA project on scaling AI compute](https://www.aria.org.uk/scaling-compute/).

My research focuses on machine learning systems across the full stack, from models and data to runtimes, systems software, and emerging AI hardware. A long-term goal of my group is to rethink the machine learning systems stack so that future AI infrastructure can achieve up to a 1000X improvement in efficiency, scalability, and reliability. Our work combines research publications with reusable [open-source systems and libraries](/#projects), which have collectively received over 20,000 GitHub stars. I also co-edited the open-source textbook [Machine Learning Systems: Design and Implementation](https://github.com/openmlsys).

Before joining Edinburgh, I was a Research Associate at [Imperial College London](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/) and a Visiting Researcher at [Microsoft Research](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/). I received my PhD under the supervision of [Paolo Costa](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/people/pcosta/) and [Alexander L. Wolf](https://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/~alw/), supported by a [Google Fellowship in Cloud Computing](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/news/113227/doc-student-awarded-prestigious-2012-google/).
