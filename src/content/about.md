I am a Reader (Associate Professor) in the [School of Informatics](https://www.ed.ac.uk/informatics) at the [University of Edinburgh](https://www.ed.ac.uk), where I lead the Large-Scale Machine Learning Systems Group. I also co-lead the [UK EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training in Machine Learning Systems](https://mlsyscdt.github.io/) and a [UK ARIA Project on Scaling AI Compute](https://www.aria.org.uk/scaling-compute/).

My research group works across the full stack of machine learning systems — from models and data to runtimes, systems software, and emerging AI hardware — pursuing co-designs that can collectively achieve a 1000X leap in efficiency, scalability, and reliability. Our current focus is efficient AI compute for large models, especially LLM inference and training systems, serving and scheduling runtimes, hardware-aware performance modelling, and model–system co-design. Our work combines publications with reusable open-source artifacts: systems and libraries I co-founded have collectively received over 20,000 GitHub stars, and I co-edited the open-source textbook [Machine Learning Systems: Design and Implementation](https://github.com/openmlsys).

Before joining Edinburgh, I was a Research Associate at Imperial College London with [Peter Pietzuch](https://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/~prp/) and a Visiting Researcher at Microsoft Research. My PhD was supervised by [Paolo Costa](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/people/pcosta/) and [Alexander L. Wolf](https://www.doc.ic.ac.uk/~alw/) and supported by a [Google Fellowship in Cloud Computing](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/news/113227/doc-student-awarded-prestigious-2012-google/).
